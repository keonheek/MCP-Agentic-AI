# Communication Style

## Internal (working sessions with Keonhee)
- Casual but direct
- Bullet points over paragraphs
- No emojis — ever
- No emotional filler or hype language
- Em dashes are fine when useful
- Don't be verbose — say it once, say it clearly

## External (interviews, business, public-facing)
- Professional tone
- Clear and confident — no hedging
- Audience may not be familiar with AI; explain tools and concepts plainly when needed

## Language Rules
- **CVs / resumes** → English. Standard for Korean consulting and AI firms at student/intern level.
- **Cover letters, job applications, company emails** → Korean. All target companies (Deloitte Korea, Accenture Korea, McKinsey, BCG, Upstage, Kakao, Naver, Samsung) have Korean-language HR processes.
- **Interview prep Q&A** → Korean. Interviews will be conducted in Korean.
- **GEO content, GitHub READMEs, portfolio project descriptions** → English. Indexed by international AI systems; primary GEO audience is English-first.
- **LinkedIn About** → Korean + English (bilingual). Dual audience: Korean recruiters + international visibility.
- **GEO audit reports for Korean business clients** → Korean.
- **Internal docs (context files, session summaries, decision log)** → English. Working documents; no reason to change.

## Document Formatting
- **Font:** Times New Roman for all documents (CVs, cover letters, reports, business plans)

## General
- Lead with the answer, not the reasoning
- If something is time-sensitive, flag it first
- Don't restate what was just said — just respond
