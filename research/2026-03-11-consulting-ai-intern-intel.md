## Key Insights for Keonhee

### Technical Preparation Priority
1. **Python + SQL** — Required by all three firms. Ensure solid fundamentals: data wrangling, SQL joins, query optimization.
2. **Modern data stack** — McKinsey QuantumBlack strongly emphasizes PySpark, Airflow, Databricks. Get hands-on experience or at least conceptual knowledge.
3. **Tableau/visualization** — Deloitte explicitly mentions Tableau; BCG values data storytelling. Practice dashboarding.
4. **Machine learning** — All firms expect foundational ML knowledge: supervised learning, model evaluation, feature engineering.

### Interview Preparation
- **BCG case interviews:** Start prep immediately. Focus on McKinsey/BCG case libraries. Practice business cases + quantitative analysis. Recommend CaseCoach or similar platforms.
- **McKinsey fit assessment:** Less explicit detail, but prepare for behavioral + technical scenario questions.
- **Deloitte:** Likely behavioral + technical coding (based on global internship patterns), but confirm with Korea office.

### Application Strategy
1. **McKinsey:** Check mckinsey.com/careers weekly for QuantumBlack Korea listings. If unavailable, consider global immersion (relocation).
2. **BCG:** Apply now (applications are open). Target December for earliest round of case interviews. Prepare case deck.
3. **Deloitte:** Contact Korea office directly; don't rely on generic deadlines. Taiwan/global programs closed in November; Korea may follow similar rhythm.

### Competitive Advantage
Keonhee's background (Business Administration + hands-on AI/agentic AI experience) is rare at student level. Emphasize:
- **Business context:** How AI applies to strategy, operations, client problems
- **Technical proof:** FinAgent, DART MCP, RAG demo demonstrate full-stack capability
- **Korean market insight:** DART knowledge, Samsung/LG understanding valuable for Korea consulting
