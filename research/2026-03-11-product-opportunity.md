## Findings

### Part 1: Cluely — The Core Insight

**What Cluely is:**
Desktop app functioning as a real-time AI copilot providing invisible, context-aware suggestions during live interactions (sales calls, interviews, meetings, exams, presentations). Operates via hidden overlay invisible to screen sharing/recordings.

**How it works:**
- **Input:** Microphone audio (speech-to-text) + screen OCR (reads code, slides, charts)
- **Processing:** LLM (GPT-4) ingests context, user's uploaded documents (PDFs, briefs), playbooks for scenarios
- **Output:** Instant prompts, rebuttals, talking points, answers in stealth overlay bypassing proctoring/keyloggers
- **Post-interaction:** Auto-generates summaries, emails, CRM syncs, engagement analytics

**Why it's successful:**
1. **Solves performance anxiety in high-stakes moments** — sales calls, interviews, exams. Users avoid manual tab-switching; AI prompts appear in real-time.
2. **Undetectability moat** — Bypasses detection via transparent graphics, hidden global shortcuts, masking tab activity. Marketing angle: "cheat on everything" manifesto attracts competitive users despite ethical debates.
3. **Multi-layer utility** — On-call coaching + post-call automation + integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot, CRM). Enterprise features (Call Shadow Mode) enable manager oversight.
4. **High-retention use case** — Users return for every high-stakes interaction (interview prep, sales cadence, exam season). Habit-forming.
5. **Network effects in niche** — Spreads via word-of-mouth in competitive communities (sales teams, interview prep, exam takers). Became $5.3M seed-funded startup.

**Pricing/Market:**
- B2B (enterprise sales teams, CRM integrations) and B2C (students, professionals)
- Freemium model with premium tiers for custom playbooks + analytics
- Detection cat-and-mouse game with proctoring software = ongoing feature arms race

**Risks noted:**
- Privacy concerns with sensitive inputs
- Detection evasion becomes unsustainable as proctoring improves
- Ethical gray area limits institutional adoption

### Part 3: Global Market Gaps (2026)

**Agentic AI for Professionals**

- **Gap:** Domain-specific reasoning systems for lawyers, accountants, consultants remain underdeveloped. General LLMs don't handle legal research workflows, financial audit procedures, or strategy consulting queries with acceptable reliability/governance.
- **Willingness to pay:** High. Professionals seek proactive agents for contract analysis, compliance checks, tax planning. $250M+ ARR potential for AI-native tools. Enterprise demand for labor automation.
- **Blocker:** Governance, cybersecurity, integration with legacy workflows (e.g., law firm practice management systems, accounting software). Pilots exist; production deployment lags.

**AI Coaching Tools**

- **Gap:** Personalized, real-time guidance evolving from passive assistants to anticipatory partners. Non-technical business users (sales, management, skill-building) lack accessible coaching tools.
- **Willingness to pay:** Medium-high. Health/wellness apps, business skills platforms gaining traction. Demand for measurable ROI (e.g., "5% productivity uplift").
- **Blocker:** Scaling to non-dev users, validating real-world outcomes, retention.

**Real-Time AI Overlays**

- **Gap:** AR/VR integrations, live data augmentations for physical/on-site work (consultant audits, lawyer depositions, field service). Infrastructure maturity enables low-latency distributed computing, but deployment beyond screens nascent.
- **Willingness to pay:** Growing, tied to $527B AI capex in 2026. High-labor-cost sectors (consulting, law, healthcare) budget for productivity tools.
- **Blocker:** Physical integration complexity, efficiency beyond datacenters, regulatory approval (e.g., in healthcare).

**Market size signal:** 50+ AI-native businesses projected to reach $250M ARR by end-2026, signaling enterprise budgets shift from pilots to production tools offsetting labor costs.

### Part 4: Korean Market Gaps (High-Value Lever)

**AI Framework Act (Jan 22, 2026) — Regulatory Tailwind**

- Mandates risk assessments, monitoring for high-impact AI systems (healthcare, finance, law enforcement)
- Requires labeling of AI-generated content
- Creates demand for governance, detection, compliance tools
- **Status:** Startups largely unprepared; corporate buyers (hospitals, insurers, law firms, fintech) adopting actively

**International tool gaps:**
- Generic global models ignore Korean data privacy + Act compliance
- Limited Hangul processing depth (speech recognition, document understanding)
- No native support for Korean law, Korean financial regs, Korean e-commerce workflows

**Sectors with high Korean spend:**
1. **Law firms** — Risk assessment dashboards, AI-powered doc review with Act compliance, detection for AI fakes in evidence
2. **Fintech** — Decision tools with mandatory risk assessments (high-impact per Act), secure processing for financial workflows
3. **Consulting** — Risk assessment/monitoring dashboards for AI strategy in semis, defense, chipmaking
4. **E-commerce** — Inventory, personalization, content labeling under Korean consumer data privacy + Act
5. **Education** — Adaptive learning with risk-monitored high-impact systems, compliant content generation

**Spending signals:**
- KRW 9.9 trillion AI R&D budget (2026), triple prior levels
- Samsung profit tripled to 20 trillion won on memory chips for Nvidia AI
- KOSPI at record highs driven by AI/semiconductors
- DeepBrain AI, FuriosaAI scaling compliant exports

**Unique Korean advantage:** Small, tight market (51M people) with high tech adoption + government backing + regulatory clarity = faster iteration cycles than global markets. A Korean-first product can scale to global (regulation following behind).

## Top 3 Product Opportunities (Solo Builder, 2026)

### Opportunity 1: Korean AI Compliance Dashboard for Law Firms & Fintech

**The Gap:**
Korean AI Framework Act requires high-impact systems (law, finance) to maintain risk assessments, monitoring logs, and AI-generated content labeling. International AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) have no compliance layer. Korean law firms + fintech + insurance firms are actively adopting AI but lack governance tools.

**The Product:**
- **AI-powered compliance dashboard** that sits between a firm's AI tools (internal LLMs, ChatGPT usage, document analysis engines) and the firm's workflows
- **Features:**
  - Auto-monitor all AI usage in the firm (APIs, internal models, ChatGPT plug-ins)
  - Detect high-risk use cases (contract analysis, financial decision-making, customer data processing)
  - Generate compliance reports for regulators (risk assessments, monitoring logs)
  - Label AI-generated vs. human-written content in documents (with audit trail)
  - Integration with law firm practice management systems (e.g., Lexis Nexis) + fintech platforms
  - **Optional:** Custom prompt library for legal use cases (e.g., "draft NDA under Korean law with compliance labels")

**Why it's a one-man build:**
- **Tech stack:** Python + LangGraph (for agentic monitoring) + FastAPI (backend for law firm integrations) + Streamlit (dashboard UI)
- **Core logic:** Simple LLM-as-judge to classify usage risk + logging + report generation
- **Domain moat:** You have Korean market knowledge + legal/financial background from Business Administration + consulting club (SDC) connections = credibility with target users
- **Validation path:** Call 5-10 Seoul law firms, pitch "compliance without overhead," validate willingness to pay (likely KRW 2-5M/month for mid-size firm)

**Market timing:**
- AI Framework Act enforcement begins now (Jan 2026)
- Law firms have budget (regulatory requirement) but no vendor
- Korean market small enough to sell direct; B2B sales motion easier than global

**Monetization:**
- SaaS: KRW 2-5M/month per law firm (usage-based or tiered)
- Target: 10-20 law firms by year-end = KRW 240-1,200M ARR
- Upsell: Custom prompt libraries, integrations with specific practice management systems

**Risks:**
- Regulatory interpretation changes
- Larger AI vendors (OpenAI, Anthropic) may bundle compliance later
- Requires domain expertise in Korean law (mitigate: hire or partner with legal advisor part-time)

### Opportunity 2: Real-Time AI Overlay for Korean Consulting & Pitch Preparation

**The Gap:**
Cluely solves the "high-stakes live scenario" problem (interviews, sales calls). But Cluely is opaque, ethical concerns, and targets English-speaking markets. A **Korean-first, transparent alternative** for consultants, business students, and job interview candidates filling the same need with a different approach.

**The Product:**
- **"CoachBot"** — Real-time AI coaching overlay for Korean language interactions
- **Features:**
  - **Live interview mode:** Whisper API (real-time speech-to-text) + LLM (GPT-4o Korean) + side panel with talking points, anticipatory questions, follow-up suggestions
  - **Pitch mode:** Live slide tracking (OCR of your Keynote/PowerPoint) + real-time engagement feedback ("slow down," "emphasize this metric")
  - **Consulting case mode:** Rapid case framework suggestions, data lookups from knowledge base (e.g., "Samsung revenue 2024: KRW 283T")
  - **Job interview prep:** Common Korean interview Q&A, company-specific talking points, evaluation feedback post-call
  - **Transparent to others:** Unlike Cluely, users explicitly share screen with interviewers if wanted (or turn off, but no "stealth" claims)
  - **Korean language focus:** Optimized for Korean phrasing, cultural cues, business etiquette
  - **Integration:** Sync coaching notes + performance video (Screenflow/OBS) → Notion database for portfolio/review

**Why it's a one-man build:**
- **Tech stack:** Python (LangGraph for coaching agent) + FastAPI (real-time API) + React (side panel UI) + Whisper API (Korean STT) + GPT-4o-mini (fast, cheap)
- **Domain expertise:** You know SKKU interview dynamics, consulting case formats, Korean business culture. Can iterate fast with student user base (free tier = viral growth)
- **Differentiation vs. Cluely:** Transparent, ethical, Korean-first, focus on skill-building (not cheating). Builds trust.

**Market timing:**
- Consulting internships heat up (spring recruiting in Korea = Feb-May)
- Job interviews peak (March-June for fall internships, Sept-Dec for next spring)
- SKKU students are early adopters; word-of-mouth spreads fast

**Monetization:**
- **Free tier:** 5 practice sessions/month (enough for casual users)
- **Pro tier:** KRW 29K/month = unlimited sessions + performance analytics + 1:1 coaching session (via partner)
- **Target:** 500-1000 SKKU students by year-end = KRW 145-290M ARR + up-sell to corporate interview coaching (consultancies, law firms)

**Risks:**
- Requires high-quality Korean language model tuning (GPT-4o-mini helps, but prompt engineering needed)
- Sticky only if users see measurable ROI (e.g., "got the job" success stories matter)
- Micro-market (SKKU + Seoul consulting circles) may be too small long-term

**Expansion path:**
- Add corporate trainer dashboard: companies (Samsung HR, Hyundai MBA programs) use for interview prep at scale
- International: English version for global consulting candidates (but deprioritize vs. Korean focus)

### Opportunity 3: Agentic Financial Analysis for Korean SME Business Planning & Pitching

**The Gap:**
Korean SMEs (small-medium enterprises) and startups pitch to VCs, banks, and government subsidies (e.g., K-Startup Grand Challenge, IITP grants). They need financial projections, competitive analysis, market sizing — but most founders don't have CFO expertise. International AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini) lack Korean market data (DART, KOSPI, regulatory filings), industry benchmarks, and agentic workflow.

**The Product:**
- **"PitchFi Agent"** — Multi-agent financial analysis for Korean startups and SMEs
- **Architecture:**
  - **Agent 1 (Data Retriever):** LangGraph agent that queries DART-FSS API (Korean corporate filings), KOSPI data, industry reports, regulatory databases
  - **Agent 2 (Financial Modeler):** Builds 3-year projections, burn rate analysis, unit economics (e.g., CAC, LTV) based on industry benchmarks (you maintain a Korean SME benchmark DB)
  - **Agent 3 (Competitive Analyst):** RAG over Korean competitor filings, market reports, news (via Perplexity API for real-time data)
  - **Agent 4 (Pitch Optimizer):** Reviews founder's pitch deck (PDF), suggests data-backed talking points, flags weak assumptions
  - **Output:** Polished financial model (Excel export) + pitch deck with AI-generated slides + 1-pager for bank/VC submission

**Why it's a one-man build:**
- **Your tech stack is perfect:** You built FinAgent (LangGraph + RAG + Text2SQL) and DART MCP server. This is a natural extension.
- **Data advantage:** DART MCP server already operational; leverage it as moat. Competitors need to build DART integration from scratch.
- **Domain expertise:** Business Administration + consulting club = credibility with target founders; you speak their language

**Market timing:**
- Government subsidies (K-Startup, IITP) open funding cycles every quarter; founders scramble to build pitches 4-6 weeks before deadline
- Post-pandemic: 5000+ Korean startups founded annually; many undercapitalized founders without finance experience
- VC/bank adoption of AI tools accelerating (due diligence automation trend)

**Monetization:**
- **Free tier:** Analyze 1 company financials/month (builder can test product)
- **Startup tier:** KRW 99K/month = unlimited analysis + pitch deck generation (10 pitches/month)
- **Pro tier:** KRW 499K/month = bespoke financial modeling + 1 live consultation (you provide via Zoom) + institutional data (proprietary Korean SME benchmarks)
- **Target:** 100-200 startups/founders by year-end = KRW 10-100M ARR
- **B2B upsell:** VCs and banks using PitchFi to auto-screen 100 incoming pitches/year = KRW 5-10M/month per institution

**Risks:**
- DART API stability + Korean regulatory changes
- Founders may distrust AI financial models without human review (mitigate: position as "draft assistant, not substitute for accountant")
- Highly competitive space (Stripe data, Carta, etc. in global market; few Korean equivalents)

**Expansion path:**
- Add scenario planning ("what if 50% slower growth?" = rebuild model)
- Partner with KBIZ (Korean Business Association) or KCIFF (Korean Crowdfunding Investors) for bulk licensing
- Sell anonymized benchmark database to VCs for due diligence
- Expand to Japan, Singapore (same tech stack, localize data feeds)
